# Spaced repetition

A review schedule that spaces study sessions at increasing intervals to move
material into long-term memory with the least total effort. Each successful
recall lets the next interval grow; a lapse shrinks it.

The technique works against the [[forgetting-curve]]: because retention decays
exponentially without review, a small, well-timed review just before the point
of forgetting resets the curve far more efficiently than re-reading.
